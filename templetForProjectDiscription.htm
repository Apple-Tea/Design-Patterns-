Mural at Intersection of Loch Raven and Aberdeen

Project Leader

Tara W

Location:

intersection of loch raven and lberdeen

Start Date

03/04/2017 at 10:30AM

Description

Let's beautify Cookeville, one wall at a time!

This year's Leadership Putnam community development project is a mural at the intersection of Washington Avenue and the new Tennessee Central Heritage Rail Trail - the bicycling and walking path that runs along the rail road tracks from the Cookeville Train Depot to Algood, TN.

The mural will demonstrate community pride, produce an emotional response in viewers, and inspire other groups to turn abandoned or underused walls in Cookeville into spaces for creative expression and beautification.

We want the mural to be colorful, celebrate diversity and incorporate transportation images of trains, trails, bicycling, and vehicles.

Our project team is interested in completing a project that will have impact on several different levels: promote art, creativity, exercise, the rail history of Cookeville, and community involvement. This is a privately owned building and the owner has been verbally agreeable to our proposal. The mural will be professionally painted with a goal of it lasting 20 plus years.

The steps

We have already applied for a grant with the Cookeville Arts Council, and we need matching funds to make this project a reality!

We hope to have an artist selected and a design finalized by April.

From May to June we will give the artist at least one month complete mural, and our team will help to recruit volunteers to assist with the project.

In July we plan to host a grand opening ceremony at the mural and invite entire community to celebrate its beauty!

Why we're doing it

Leadership Putnam is a local civic organization affiliated with the Cookeville Chamber of Commerce. Every year members of Leadership Putnam work on projects to better our local community. These projects are designed to promote local business, industry and tourism in this area and to improve quality of life and resources in Putnam County.
